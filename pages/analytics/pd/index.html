📚 Basics: Understanding PD Patterns

What is Partial Discharge?

Partial Discharge (PD) is a localized discharge in insulation system that does not bridge the entire insulation. Although single discharge energy is small, long-term accumulation leads to insulation deterioration and eventual breakdown.

Basic PRPD Pattern Types

TypePhase FeatureAmplitudeTypical Cause
Internal DischargeSymmetric in 0-90 and 180-270 degConcentratedVoid, delamination
Surface DischargeConcentrated in one phase regionHigh dispersionSurface contamination
Corona DischargeFixed at 90 or 270 degStableSharp points, burrs
Floating DischargePhase drift, no fixed regionHigh dispersionPoor grounding

🔬 Professional: Analysis Methods

1. PRPD Pattern Analysis

Phase-Resolved Partial Discharge (PRPD) displays discharge pulses distributed by power frequency phase.

2. PRPS Pattern Analysis

Phase-Resolved Pulse Sequence adds time dimension to observe discharge sequence characteristics.
